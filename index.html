How He Proposed

As told by Ryan

On July 24th, Nancy, Michelle and I woke up early to meet Patrick at Point Dume. We all arrived at the location around 8 a.m. to prepare. Rachel texted me that she was arriving with Brenda and the rest of her friends and all of us went to our hiding spots. However, unexpected guests kept coming over to the viewing point to take pictures and I even had one person come up and ask me what I was doing under the platform. As soon as I saw the platform empty of strangers, I started playing our song (Thinking Out Loud by Ed Sheeran) and the girls walked away and left Brenda alone. Brenda was confused and when she saw me under the platform she originally thought I was a bum. When she realized it was me she knew what was going on. I then got down on my left knee and asked her to marry me. That day was the best day of my life because I knew that I was going to spend the rest of my life with my best friend.

Maid of Honor:
Nancy Estrada

I’m honored to have my sister Nancy be my maid of honor. Nancy has been working in Spain as an English teacher for the past year and graduated from CSU Long Beach in 2016 with a BA in History. She is intelligent, independent, and pretty feisty (she gets it from my dad’s side). It’s been hard not seeing her for almost a year but I’m happy she’s enjoying seeing the world while she’s still young. Thankfully, she’ll be home during the summer to help out with the rest of the wedding planning and whip everyone in our wedding party into shape with that feisty attitude of hers.

Bridesmaid:
Michelle Estrada

Michelle is the youngest of us Estrada girls and has lived with Ryan and I for the last two years. She’s the baby of the family and I’m very overprotective of her, but I do it out of love. Michelle is a free spirit who gets along with everyone and has an infectious laugh. She immediately makes friends with anyone she meets and has a great sense of humor--I love that about her. She hopes to get her Bachelor’s degree in American Sign Language/Deaf Studies and I know that she’ll do great things and touch many lives along the way.

Bridesmaid:
Rachel Sevilla

Rachel is one of my best friends and we have quite a funny story. We immediately became best buds in the 3rd grade because we were the top two students in our class. One of my fondest memories of Rachel is when she came to my 11th birthday party and gave me an illustrated children’s bible (probably the only bible I’ve read, ha). After 3rd grade, Rachel moved away, but little did I know that our paths would cross again in high school. We rekindled our friendship in 11th grade and it was like old times. Through thick and thin, Rachel has always been there for me--moving, breakups, and new beginnings. She is so funny, intelligent, and thoughtful, and an incredible friend.

Bridesmaid:
Deidre Moseley

Deidre is also one of my best friends and I met her through Rachel my junior year of high school. We quickly became really good friends and were in a couple of classes together our senior year. I’ll never forget our hilarious video project we worked on together in our 12th grade Mythology course. After we graduated high school, Deidre moved up to San Francisco for college, but we would always hang out when she came back home during the summer and throughout the years our friendship has never wavered. Deidre is so caring, has the biggest heart, and is a very sentimental person. I really admire her as a person and her character and compassion continually inspire me.

Bridesmaid:
Natalie Bechai

I met Natalie back in 8th grade when she moved to Castaic and we became really close our freshman year when we were on the VHS swim team together. We remained friends through high school and community college. We’d spend countless hours studying together for Microbiology and Anatomy & Physiology and I always remember Natalie would get me through long hours in the library with all the snacks she’d pack for the day. Natalie followed her dream of becoming an RN and now lives in Las Vegas with her fiance, Vito, but always texts me to keep in touch--and it’s rare to have friends like that nowadays. I will always be grateful to her and Vito for introducing me to Ryan.

Best Man:
Patrick Sarkis

Patrick is my younger brother but he is more than that. He knows me better than anyone else. We’ve been through it all together: running around in our pajamas, the crazy fights we had as kids, him destroying my Kobe Basketball cards after beating him in basketball to name a few. I have so many memories I could share but I could never put into words how much he means to me. I would do anything for my brother. He has been my one constant my entire life. We are very different but yet so much alike. Regardless how annoying he can be I could not picture my life without him.

Groomsman:
Jacob Lovelady

My cousin Jacob is my oldest friend and my first best friend. I would spend a week every summer at my Aunt Kate’s and Uncle Ron’s house and always looked forward to doing fun things with Jacob like playing Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles and going to the Wave Waterpark. One thing about Jake is he never stops thinking. He has always been an entrepreneur from raising pigs to starting his own company in Colombia. I look up to him in many ways but the one that sticks out to me is the way he treats people. Jake is the most respectful person I know, is a great husband, and is always encouraging people to do good things and be the best they can be.

Groomsman:
Vito Panarelli

Vito is one of my oldest friends and our friendship goes back to the basketball courts at Valencia High during our freshman year. From day one Vito treated me like a younger brother. He would pick on me and tease me but more importantly he took me under his wing and always had my back. Vito and our friend Alex are responsible for my legendary nickname Pooty. To this day the name has stuck. When he calls me by my first name it just doesn't sound right. Even after high school and to this day he treats me the same way. The name is Pooty spin it backwards Ytoop.

Groomsman:
Matthew Stansbery

I met Matt back in 2005. After my first impression of him I never thought we would become the close friends we are today. He came off kind of dorky and had a lot of energy. After getting to know Matt, I learned that we had a lot in common such as music, sports, and end up in funny situations together. He became someone I could always depend on and was always supportive of my decisions. We have had our up and downs but no matter what we have always respected each other and have been there for each other when it counts.

Groomsman:
David Rogers

Now for Mr. Rogers. My first memory of David was him throwing pumpkins down my street on Halloween when we were in Middle School. Even though we knew each other from school, we didn't become good friends until we worked together at Sears in Valencia. The things he would pull there would drive me nuts. What really brought our friendship together was GGGGG G-Unit. Every time a member of G-Unit released an album we would always go to Best Buy, then go to his house to listen to the entire thing. The things I cherish about our friendship are his loyalty and his willingness to drop everything to help you out.
