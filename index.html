Our Story

How We Met: As told by Brenda

Ryan and I were set up on a blind date by two of our good friends, Natalie and Vito, in February of 2014. We had agreed that I would meet them at Vito's house and the four of us would go out on a double date for dinner and drinks. I really wasn't interested in dating during that time in my life and was trying to find any excuse not to go. (I mean, who really enjoys blind dates?) Natalie knew there was a good chance I would cancel, so she showed up to my work that day to make sure I wouldn't flake. I remember driving over to Vito's after work wanting to get the night over with. We were awkwardly introduced and kind of got off to a bad start because I was annoyed that Ryan didn't open the car door for me (he apparently was very nervous). The first hour was followed by small talk during drinks at BJ's and I felt like he was ignoring me. I excused myself to go to the bathroom and Natalie followed to ask me what I thought about Ryan. I told her I thought he was attractive but he didn't seem interested. Of course, our little matchmaker Natalie made sure to pull Ryan aside that evening to give him a pep talk because he mutually thought the same thing. After getting the inside scoop-and these are Ryan's words, not mine-it was "game over" for him. We exchanged numbers and went on a hike the next day and we went on our first real date a week later. Ryan took me to his favorite childhood restaurant, Miceli's, in Studio City, followed by drinks and dancing at The Sayers Club in Hollywood. A few months later, Ryan officially asked me to be his girlfriend. I knew very early on that Ryan was special and that I had found the man I wanted to spend the rest of my life with.

How He Proposed

As told by Ryan

The idea of me proposing to Brenda happened while I was playing basketball. I was shooting around thinking of how much I love her and how I couldn't picture my life without her. I had many thoughts go through my head on how I wanted to propose to her. I wanted it to be a special moment that Brenda and I would never forget. I originally planned to propose to her at Towsley Canyon which was the first place we spent time alone together when we went on a hike the day after we were introduced. I reached out to Brenda's good friend Rachel to get her input. She expressed so much emotion and excitement for Brenda and I and came up with the perfect idea of using her birthday as a distraction. They would have a girls' day and sleepover, then go on a hike to Towsley next morning. However, mother nature got in the way of my plan. There was a huge fire the weekend of the proposal and Towsley Canyon was closed. I thought this was a sign to postpone everything, but Nancy, Michelle, and Rachel were reassuring. Nancy came up with the idea to go check out a couple more hiking sites in Malibu, and Point Dume was perfect. I found a viewing platform that looked over the ocean where I could hide and surprise Brenda.

On July 24th, Nancy, Michelle and I woke up early to meet Patrick at Point Dume. We all arrived at the location around 8 a.m. to prepare. Rachel texted me that she was arriving with Brenda and the rest of her friends and all of us went to our hiding spots. However, unexpected guests kept coming over to the viewing point to take pictures and I even had one person come up and ask me what I was doing under the platform. As soon as I saw the platform empty of strangers, I started playing our song (Thinking Out Loud by Ed Sheeran) and the girls walked away and left Brenda alone. Brenda was confused and when she saw me under the platform she originally thought I was a bum. When she realized it was me she knew what was going on. I then got down on my left knee and asked her to marry me. That day was the best day of my life because I knew that I was going to spend the rest of my life with my best friend.
